## Introduction

While herbs are widely enjoyed as natural plant-based remedies, caution is needed when combining them with pharmaceutical medications. The following herbs in particular have documented drug interactions.

## St. John's Wort

St. John's Wort enhances the activity of drug-metabolizing enzymes in the liver, which can affect the efficacy of certain medications. Caution is required when taking it alongside:

- **Indinavir** (anti-HIV drug)
- **Digoxin** (cardiac glycoside)
- **Ciclosporin** (immunosuppressant)
- **Theophylline** (bronchodilator)
- **Warfarin** (anticoagulant)
- **Oral contraceptives**

By accelerating the metabolism of these drugs, St. John's Wort can lower their blood concentrations and reduce their effectiveness.

## Ginkgo Leaf

Ginkgo leaf has platelet-activating factor inhibiting properties, which means **caution is needed when combining it with anticoagulants** such as Warfarin. This combination may increase the risk of bleeding.

## Using Herbs Safely

- If you are taking any medications, always consult your doctor or pharmacist before incorporating herbs
- Concentrated herbal supplements carry a higher risk of interactions than herbal teas
- Enjoying herbs as tea in normal quantities is generally safe, but avoid excessive consumption
