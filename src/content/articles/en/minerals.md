## What Are Minerals?

About 95% of the human body is composed of four main elements: hydrogen, oxygen, carbon, and nitrogen. The remaining elements are known as minerals (inorganic substances), making up about 5% of body weight.

Since minerals cannot be synthesized in the body, they must be obtained from food. However, excessive intake can also cause health problems. Minerals exist as electrolytes (ions) in the body, playing roles in osmotic pressure regulation and biological function regulation.

## Key Minerals and Their Functions

### Calcium (Ca)
Involved in bone and tooth formation, mental stability, and blood coagulation.

### Iron (Fe)
A component of hemoglobin, which carries oxygen throughout the body. Vitamin C enhances its absorption, while tannins inhibit it.

### Phosphorus (P)
A structural component of bones, teeth, and cell membranes. Also part of high-energy compounds like ATP and nucleic acids.

### Magnesium (Mg)
Involved in enzyme activation, bone structure, mental stability, temperature and blood pressure regulation, and cardiac function.

### Potassium (K)
Plays roles in nerve transmission, muscle contraction, osmotic pressure maintenance, and kidney waste excretion.

### Copper (Cu)
Found in bones, skeletal muscle, blood, and liver. Involved in hemoglobin synthesis.

### Iodine (I)
A component of thyroid hormones, involved in protein synthesis, enzyme reactions, and nerve cell development.

### Manganese (Mn)
Involved in the metabolism of the three major nutrients and as a component of antioxidant enzymes that break down free radicals.

### Selenium (Se)
A component of antioxidant enzymes that helps prevent aging and cancer by breaking down free radicals.

### Zinc (Zn)
A component of many enzymes, involved in nucleic acid and protein synthesis, and normalization of taste and smell.

### Chromium (Cr)
Enhances insulin action and is involved in sugar and lipid metabolism.

### Molybdenum (Mo)
Involved in uric acid metabolism and is a component of enzymes that break down toxic substances.

### Sodium (Na)
Involved in nerve transmission, muscle contraction, and maintenance of body fluid osmotic pressure.

## Summary

Since minerals cannot be produced by the body, obtaining them through a balanced diet is essential. Both deficiency and excess can affect health, so appropriate intake is important. Many herbal teas are also rich in minerals, making them a helpful part of daily supplementation.
