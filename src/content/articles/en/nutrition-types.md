## Nutrient Classification

The nutrients we obtain from food can be classified into progressively larger groups:

### 3 Major Nutrients
- **Carbohydrates (sugars)**
- **Lipids (fats)**
- **Proteins**

### 5 Major Nutrients
Adding to the three above:
- **Vitamins**
- **Minerals**

### 6 Major Nutrients
Adding:
- **Dietary fiber** (a type of carbohydrate)

### 7 Major Nutrients
Adding:
- **Phytochemicals** (plant chemical compounds)

## Three Functions of Nutrients

### Building the Body (Structural)
**Key nutrients:** Lipids, Proteins, Minerals

These serve as the building materials for all body tissues — blood, bones, teeth, muscles, organs, hormones, and enzymes.

### Producing Energy (Caloric)
**Key nutrients:** Carbohydrates, Lipids, Proteins

These provide the heat and energy needed to power the body. They are essential for daily activities and sustaining life.

### Regulating Body Functions (Regulatory)
**Key nutrients:** Proteins, Vitamins, Minerals

These regulate bodily functions and metabolism, helping to maintain immune function and assist in the metabolism of other nutrients.

## Herbs and Nutrition

Many herbal teas are rich in vitamins, minerals, and phytochemicals. Incorporating herbs into your daily diet can help supplement nutrients that may be lacking in your regular meals.
