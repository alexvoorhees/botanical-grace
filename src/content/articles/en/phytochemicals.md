## What Are Phytochemicals?

The word "phytochemical" comes from the Greek "phyto" (plant) and "chemical" (chemical substance). **Phytochemicals are chemical compounds produced by plants** — naturally occurring substances derived from the plant kingdom.

## Why Plants Produce Phytochemicals

Plants create these compounds to protect themselves from threats like ultraviolet radiation, insects, and harmful substances. Since plants cannot move to escape danger, phytochemicals are defensive substances acquired through long evolutionary processes.

## Key Characteristics of Phytochemicals

- A single herb can contain **hundreds of different phytochemical compounds**
- They exist as **pigments, aromatic compounds, and flavor compounds** in plants
- Unlike vitamins and minerals, they are classified as **non-nutritive substances**
- Yet they contribute significantly to our **health, beauty, and anti-aging** efforts

## Major Types of Phytochemicals

### Polyphenols
Plant pigment and astringency compounds with strong antioxidant properties. This group includes flavonoids, tannins, and catechins.

### Terpenoids
Aromatic plant compounds found abundantly in essential oils. They offer relaxation effects and antimicrobial properties.

### Carotenoids
Red, yellow, and orange pigment compounds. Representative examples include beta-carotene and lycopene, both known for their antioxidant activity.

## Scientific Evidence

Research has demonstrated that phytochemicals help maintain the body's defense functions and homeostasis. Recent studies continue to reveal the extensive health benefits of these plant-derived compounds.
