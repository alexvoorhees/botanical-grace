Artichoke (Cynara scolymus) is a perennial plant from the Asteraceae family, native to the Mediterranean region. It has been used as a medicinal herb since ancient Greek and Roman times. The leaves contain key active compounds such as cynarin and chlorogenic acid, which are known to support liver function and promote bile secretion.

Artichoke is highly valued for its digestive properties, traditionally used to relieve bloating and indigestion after meals. Research has also shown its potential to support healthy cholesterol metabolism and maintain balanced blood lipid levels. The bitter compounds in artichoke stimulate the secretion of digestive juices and can help improve appetite.

In herbal therapy, artichoke is commonly consumed as a tea or tincture. It is frequently included in blends designed for liver detoxification and digestive support, making it one of the most important herbs in modern phytotherapy practice.
