Calendula (Calendula officinalis) is an annual plant from the Asteraceae family, native to Southern Europe, known for its vibrant orange flowers. Also called pot marigold, it has been valued as a medicinal plant since medieval Europe.

The petals contain carotenoids, flavonoids, and saponins that provide excellent wound-healing properties. Calendula promotes the recovery of cuts, abrasions, and minor burns while supporting skin regeneration. Its anti-inflammatory action helps calm symptoms of eczema and dermatitis, and it strengthens the skin's protective barrier function.

In herbal therapy, calendula is used externally as an infused oil, cream, or ointment, and is also consumed internally as a tea. As one of the premier skincare herbs, it is gentle enough to be used on all ages, from babies to adults.
