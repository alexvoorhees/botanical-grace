Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) is an evergreen shrub from the Ericaceae family, native to North America, producing tart red berries. It has been used by Native Americans for both food and medicine, and today it is recognized worldwide as a valuable functional food.

Cranberry is best known for its role in preventing urinary tract infections (UTIs). The proanthocyanidins (PACs) in the fruit have been scientifically shown to prevent bacteria such as E. coli from adhering to the urinary tract lining. Additionally, cranberry is rich in antioxidant compounds including polyphenols and vitamin C, which protect cells from oxidative stress. It also possesses antibacterial properties that contribute to oral health.

In herbal therapy, cranberry is consumed in various forms including juice, capsules, and dried fruit. It is particularly well-recognized as a herb that supports urinary tract health in women and is commonly incorporated as a preventive daily care practice.
