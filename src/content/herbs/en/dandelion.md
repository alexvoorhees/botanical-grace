Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) is a perennial plant from the Asteraceae family found throughout the world. It has been used as a versatile medicinal herb across European, Asian, and Native American traditional medicine systems for centuries.

Dandelion leaves are rich in potassium and possess excellent diuretic properties. Unlike conventional diuretics, dandelion replenishes potassium while promoting urination, earning it recognition as "nature's diuretic." The root supports liver function and promotes bile secretion to aid detoxification. Additionally, its bitter compounds stimulate the secretion of digestive juices, improving digestion and appetite.

In herbal therapy, the leaves are used as a tea or in salads, while the roots can be roasted as a coffee substitute or prepared as a tincture. Dandelion plays a central role in detox and cleanse programs, making it a common yet highly valuable medicinal plant.
