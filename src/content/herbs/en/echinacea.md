Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea) is a perennial plant from the Asteraceae family, native to North America. It was traditionally used by Native Americans to treat infections and wounds, and today it is one of the most widely used immune-supporting herbs worldwide.

The active compounds in echinacea, including alkylamides, polysaccharides, and caffeic acid derivatives, stimulate the innate immune system and enhance white blood cell activity. It is widely used to prevent and reduce the severity of colds and influenza, with confirmed antiviral and antibacterial properties. Taking echinacea at the early onset of infection is believed to help prevent symptoms from becoming severe.

In herbal therapy, echinacea is available in many forms including tea, tincture, and capsules. It is particularly popular as a key ingredient in blended teas during cold and flu season, and is widely used as a daily herbal care practice for strengthening immune resilience.
