Elderflower (Sambucus nigra) is a deciduous shrub from the Adoxaceae family, native to Europe. It produces clusters of small, cream-colored flowers in early summer. In European traditional medicine, elderflower has been called "the medicine chest of the people" and has been used in folk remedies for centuries.

The most important action of elderflower is its diaphoretic property, which promotes sweating during the early stages of colds and influenza to support the body's natural healing response. It also has diuretic properties that help eliminate excess fluid from the body, aiding in the reduction of swelling. Its anti-catarrhal action soothes inflammation of the nasal and throat mucous membranes, making it useful for alleviating hay fever and sinusitis symptoms.

In herbal therapy, elderflower is often enjoyed as a tea with a sweet, muscat-like aroma, and is known as a pleasant herb suitable even for children. A traditional preparation involves blending elderflower with peppermint and yarrow as a cold-relief tea.
