Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) is a perennial plant from the Apiaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region. Its sweet-scented seeds (fruits) are used medicinally. It has been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes since ancient Egyptian, Greek, and Roman times.

The essential oil compounds in fennel seeds, including anethole and fenchone, act on the smooth muscles of the digestive tract to promote digestion. It is particularly valued for its carminative action, effectively relieving bloating and abdominal distension. Fennel has been traditionally used to ease infant colic. It also possesses estrogenic properties and is known as a galactagogue that promotes breast milk production.

In herbal therapy, the seeds are lightly crushed and brewed as a tea. It is widely used as an after-meal digestive tea and as a support tea for breastfeeding mothers. Its sweet, gentle flavor makes it pleasant to drink and well-suited for blending with other herbs.
