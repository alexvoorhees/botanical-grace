Flaxseed (Linum usitatissimum) is an annual plant from the Linaceae family, native to Central Asia, also known as linseed. It is considered one of the first plants cultivated by humans, with both its fiber and seeds used since ancient times.

The abundant mucilage in flaxseed absorbs water and swells, promoting intestinal peristalsis through a gentle laxative action. It is widely used as a natural remedy for constipation relief. Flaxseed is also rich in omega-3 fatty acids (alpha-linolenic acid), providing anti-inflammatory benefits throughout the body. Its lignans and dietary fiber contribute to lowering blood cholesterol levels, supporting cardiovascular health.

In herbal therapy, the seeds are consumed whole or ground, typically added to yogurt or smoothies. Flaxseed oil is also available in capsule form. As a highly functional herb that is easy to incorporate into daily meals, flaxseed is widely used in modern health management.
