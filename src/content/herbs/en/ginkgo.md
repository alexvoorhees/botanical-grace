Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba) is a deciduous tree often called a "living fossil," as it has existed on Earth for approximately 200 million years. Long used in traditional Chinese medicine, it has become one of the most extensively researched herbs in modern European phytotherapy.

The leaves contain flavonoid glycosides and terpene lactones (ginkgolides and bilobalide), which promote peripheral blood circulation and improve blood flow to the brain and extremities. These properties are believed to contribute to improved memory, concentration, and overall cognitive function. Ginkgo also provides powerful antioxidant protection, helping to shield cells from free radical damage.

In herbal therapy, ginkgo is commonly used as a standardized extract or tea. It is particularly popular for supporting cognitive function in older adults, improving cold extremities, and alleviating tinnitus symptoms.
