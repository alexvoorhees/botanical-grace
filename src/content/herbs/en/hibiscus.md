Hibiscus (Hibiscus sabdariffa) is an annual plant from the Malvaceae family, native to West Africa, also known as roselle. The vibrant red calyces are used medicinally and as a beverage. In Egypt and Sudan, it has been traditionally enjoyed as "karkade" for centuries.

The plant acids abundant in hibiscus, including citric acid, malic acid, and hibiscus acid, boost metabolism and help with fatigue recovery. Rich in antioxidant compounds such as anthocyanins and polyphenols, hibiscus protects cells from oxidative damage and supports both beauty and health. It also has gentle diuretic properties that help reduce swelling and eliminate excess fluid from the body.

In herbal therapy, hibiscus is enjoyed as a beautiful ruby-red tea with a characteristically tart and refreshing taste. It can be served hot or iced, making it a popular refreshment tea during summer months. Its classic pairing with rosehip creates a blend that is excellent for vitamin C supplementation.
