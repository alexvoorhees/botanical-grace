Linden (Tilia europaea) is a deciduous tree from the Tiliaceae family, native to Europe. It produces sweet-scented, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. Known as "tilleul" in France, it is one of the most beloved herbal teas in Europe. In medieval Europe, it was known as "the tree with a thousand uses."

The flavonoids (such as quercetin and kaempferol), mucilage, and essential oil compounds in linden flowers act gently on the central nervous system, providing a calming effect that eases tension and anxiety. It promotes relaxation of both mind and body and helps improve stress-related insomnia. Linden also promotes sweating to help relieve early cold symptoms, and its gentle diuretic action supports the body's fluid metabolism.

In herbal therapy, the flowers and bracts are commonly brewed as a tea. With its sweet, honey-like aroma and gentle flavor, linden tea is ideal as a bedtime relaxation tea and is also suitable for children's cold care. It remains a beloved staple of European household remedies to this day.
