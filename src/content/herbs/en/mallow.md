Mallow (Malva sylvestris) is a perennial plant from the Malvaceae family, native to Europe and Western Asia, known for its beautiful purple flowers. It has a long history of use as both a food and medicinal plant since ancient Greek times.

The flowers and leaves are rich in mucilage, which is the herb's most notable characteristic. This substance creates a protective coating over the mucous membranes of the mouth, throat, and digestive tract. Mallow has been traditionally used to soothe dry, sore throats, calm coughs, and treat mouth ulcers. It also offers gentle anti-inflammatory properties that help calm inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract.

In herbal therapy, mallow is commonly enjoyed as a tea. When brewed, it produces a striking blue infusion that turns pink when acid (such as lemon juice) is added. It is used for respiratory and digestive care, and its beautiful color also makes it a popular ingredient in blended herbal teas.
