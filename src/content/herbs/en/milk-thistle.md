Milk Thistle (Silybum marianum) is an annual or biennial plant from the Asteraceae family, native to the Mediterranean region. Its name comes from the white vein-like markings on its leaves, reminiscent of milk. It has been used for liver protection for over 2,000 years and is one of the most extensively researched medicinal herbs.

The seeds contain silymarin, a complex of silibinin, silychristin, and silydianin, which stabilizes liver cell membranes and prevents the entry of toxins, thereby protecting the liver. It also promotes protein synthesis in damaged liver cells, aiding liver regeneration. Its powerful antioxidant action reduces oxidative damage to the liver and supports detoxification from alcohol, drugs, and environmental toxins.

In herbal therapy, milk thistle is commonly taken as capsules or tinctures, and the crushed seeds can also be used as a tea. It is globally recognized as the first-choice herb for liver care and is listed in the German Commission E Monographs, underscoring its credibility as a trusted medicinal herb.
