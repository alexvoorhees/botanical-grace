Mulberry (Morus alba) is a deciduous tree from the Moraceae family, native to China. In Japan, it has been cultivated for centuries for silkworm rearing, and its leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine under the name "sang ye," holding an important place in East Asian traditional healthcare.

The leaves contain deoxynojirimycin (DNJ), which inhibits sugar absorption in the small intestine. Research has shown it can suppress rapid rises in blood sugar levels after meals, making it a focus of attention for blood sugar management and preventive care for diabetes. Mulberry leaves are also rich in antioxidant compounds such as flavonoids and chlorophyll, which help prevent cellular aging. The mineral content, including iron, calcium, and zinc, provides comprehensive nourishing and tonic benefits.

In herbal therapy, the dried leaves are commonly consumed as a tea. Drinking it before meals is believed to moderate blood sugar spikes, and in Japan it is widely enjoyed as a health tea. Its mild flavor makes it easy to incorporate into daily routines.
