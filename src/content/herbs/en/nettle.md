Nettle (Urtica dioica) is a perennial plant from the Urticaceae family, native to Europe, characterized by stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. Known as the "stinging plant," its irritating effect disappears when dried, and it has been used worldwide as a highly nutritious medicinal plant since ancient times.

Nettle's most notable action is its anti-allergy effect. It contains compounds that inhibit histamine release, making it useful for relieving symptoms of hay fever and allergic rhinitis. It also has potassium-sparing diuretic properties that promote the elimination of excess fluid and waste from the body. Rich in iron, silicon, vitamin C, and chlorophyll, nettle has blood-purifying properties that help improve anemia.

In herbal therapy, nettle is commonly consumed as a daily tea and is a staple ingredient in springtime detox tea blends. It is also valued as a tonic herb for nutritional support and has traditionally been used to provide nutritional assistance during pregnancy.
