Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) is a perennial climbing vine from the Passifloraceae family, native to the southern United States, producing uniquely beautiful flowers. The name "passion" refers to the Passion of Christ, as the flower's structure was thought to resemble the cross and nails. Native Americans have long used it as a sedative.

The flavonoids in passionflower, including chrysin and apigenin, act on GABA receptors to gently calm the nervous system. It excels in its anxiolytic action, relieving anxiety and tension without the dependency or morning grogginess often associated with pharmaceutical alternatives. It is also effective for improving sleep difficulties such as trouble falling asleep or nighttime waking, supporting a natural sleep cycle.

In herbal therapy, passionflower is used as a tea or tincture. It can be blended with valerian and linden to enhance relaxation effects, and continues to play an important role in modern self-care as a gentle herb for managing the stresses of contemporary life.
