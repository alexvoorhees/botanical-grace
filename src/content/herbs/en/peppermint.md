Peppermint (Mentha x piperita) is a natural hybrid of watermint and spearmint, belonging to the Lamiaceae family. Known for its characteristic cooling menthol aroma, it is one of the most widely used herbs in the world. It has been employed for digestive complaints since ancient Egyptian, Greek, and Roman times.

Menthol, the primary active compound in peppermint, has an antispasmodic effect that relaxes the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, relieving abdominal pain and cramping. It normalizes gastrointestinal motility and promotes digestion, and is also used to improve symptoms of post-meal discomfort and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The antibacterial properties of menthol support oral hygiene and help prevent bad breath.

In herbal therapy, peppermint is most commonly used as a tea and is a classic after-meal digestif. The essential oil is also used in aromatherapy to relieve headaches and nasal congestion. Its refreshing flavor serves as a base note in many blended teas, making it a favorite among those new to herbal teas.
