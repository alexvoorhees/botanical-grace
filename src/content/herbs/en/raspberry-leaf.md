Raspberry Leaf (Rubus idaeus) is a deciduous shrub from the Rosaceae family, native to Europe and North America. Separate from the raspberry fruit, its leaves are used as a medicinal herb. In Europe, it has been cherished by expectant mothers for centuries as the "herb for easy birth."

The alkaloid fragarine found in raspberry leaf has a regulatory effect on uterine muscles, toning the uterus and supporting preparation for childbirth. The tannins provide astringent properties that help tone mucous membranes and relieve mild diarrhea. Rich in iron, calcium, magnesium, B vitamins, and vitamin C, it is also an excellent herb for comprehensive nutritional support.

In herbal therapy, raspberry leaf is most commonly consumed as a tea. Traditionally, women in late pregnancy (generally from 32 weeks onward) drink it to prepare for labor. It is also used to relieve menstrual pain and support overall women's health. It has a mild, pleasant flavor that is easy to enjoy daily.
