Saw Palmetto (Serenoa repens) is a small palm from the Arecaceae family, native to southeastern North America, producing dark purple berries. Native Americans used it to treat urinary and reproductive conditions, and today it is particularly known as a herb that supports men's health.

The fatty acids and phytosterols in saw palmetto berries inhibit the enzyme 5-alpha-reductase, which reduces the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT). This mechanism helps improve symptoms associated with benign prostatic hyperplasia, such as difficulty urinating. Its anti-inflammatory properties also help soothe inflammation in the prostate and urinary system, while contributing to hormonal balance.

In herbal therapy, saw palmetto is primarily taken as capsules or softgels. It is used by middle-aged and older men to maintain prostate health, and in some European countries it is recognized as an approved phytomedicine, making it a flagship herb of evidence-based phytotherapy.
