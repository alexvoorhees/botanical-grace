St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is a perennial plant from the Hypericaceae family, native to Europe. It blooms with yellow flowers around the summer solstice, and its name derives from St. John's Day (June 24th). Since ancient Greek times, it has been known as the "sunshine herb" and used for emotional well-being.

Its characteristic active compounds, hypericin and hyperforin, inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and noradrenaline. Clinical research has demonstrated its effectiveness in improving mild to moderate depressive symptoms. It also has gentle sedative properties that help relieve anxiety and tension, along with confirmed antiviral activity, making it a herb with multifaceted pharmacological actions.

In herbal therapy, St. John's Wort is taken internally as a tea, tincture, or capsule. The infused oil is also used externally for nerve pain and muscle aches. However, it has significant interactions with many pharmaceutical medications, so consultation with a qualified practitioner is recommended before use.
