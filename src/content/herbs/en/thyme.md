Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) is an evergreen subshrub from the Lamiaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region, widely known as a culinary herb. It has a history spanning thousands of years, from embalming in ancient Egypt to incense in ancient Greek temples.

The key active compounds in thyme, thymol and carvacrol, possess powerful antibacterial and antifungal properties that are particularly effective against respiratory infections. Thyme combines a cough-suppressing action that relaxes bronchial muscles with an expectorant effect that helps clear mucus from the airways. It has been traditionally used to treat bronchitis and upper respiratory infections.

In herbal therapy, thyme is used as a tea, tincture, or inhalation. It is an essential herb in blended teas for relieving cold and cough symptoms, and is also used as a gargle for oral hygiene. With both culinary and medicinal applications, thyme is a versatile herb worthy of a place in every household.
