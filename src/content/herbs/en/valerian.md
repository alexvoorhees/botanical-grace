Valerian (Valeriana officinalis) is a perennial plant from the Caprifoliaceae family, native to Europe and northern Asia. It has been used as "nature's tranquilizer" since ancient Greek and Roman times. The root is characterized by its distinctive strong odor, which is itself related to the herb's pharmacological activity.

The root contains valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which act on the central nervous system by inhibiting the breakdown of GABA, producing a strong sedative effect. Valerian is one of the most widely used herbs for improving insomnia, and numerous clinical studies have demonstrated its ability to enhance sleep quality and reduce the time needed to fall asleep. It also has antispasmodic properties that relieve muscle tension and cramping.

In herbal therapy, valerian is commonly used as a tincture or capsule. While it can be consumed as a tea, its strong smell makes it an acquired taste. Blending with hops and passionflower can create synergistic sleep-supporting effects, and in Europe, valerian is widely recognized as a traditional herbal remedy for promoting sleep.
